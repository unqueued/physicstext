Problem

Is energy always conserved when two waves interfere? Explain.

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    Waves interfere either constructively or destructively. At the destructive interference resultant amplitude of the stationary wave is zero. These are called nodal regions.

    At the constructive interference the resultant amplitude is greater than the amplitude of the individual waves. These are called anti nodal regions. So, energy decreases at the nodal regions and energy increases at anti-nodal regions. Thus energy is redistributed in between these regions and total energy remains constant.

    Hence, energy is always conserved when two waves interfere.
