Problem

Why can you make water slosh back and forth in a pan only if you shake the pan at a certain frequency?

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    To make the water “slosh” we must shake the water and pan at the natural frequency for the water waves in the pan. When you shake the pan there will be waves traveling in both directions, and the waves traveling down will interfere with the waves reflected back. The water then is in resonance or in standing wave pattern and the amplitude of oscillation gets larger. Such right frequency is determined by the size of the pan, the smaller pans produce slosh at higher frequencies corresponding to shorter wavelengths for the standing waves. So water can be made to slosh back and forth in a pan only if you shake the pan at a certain frequency.
