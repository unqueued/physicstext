Problem

“In a round bowl of water, waves move from the center to the rim, or from the rim to the center, depending on whether you strike at the center or at the rim.” So wrote Dante Alighieri 700 years ago in his great poem Paradiso (Canto 14), the last part of his famous Divine Comedy. Try this experiment and discuss your results.

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    The waves are produced well in both cases. When struck the bowl at the center, then wave travels from center to the rim and reflected at the rim and moves back to the center, similarly if it is struck on the rim the wave travels from the rim to the center and reflects back. So, where ever it struck the formation of the wave pattern is similar.
