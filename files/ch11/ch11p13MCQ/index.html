Problem

What happens when two waves, such as waves on a lake, come from different directions and run into each other?

(a) They cancel each other out and disappear.

(b) If they are the same size, they cancel each other out and disappear. If one wave is larger than the other, the smaller one disappears and the larger one shrinks but continues.

(c) They get larger where they run into each other; then they continue in a direction between the direction of the two original waves and larger than either original wave.

(d) They may have various patterns where they overlap, but each wave continues with its original pattern away from the region of overlap.

(e) Waves cannot run into each other; they always come from the same direction and so are parallel.

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    Principle of superposition states that when two waves are travelling through the same point of the medium simultaneously, then the resultant displacement of the particle is algebraic sum of the displacement due to individual waves. So the waves have various patterns at the position of overlap and they continue with their original path form the region of overlap.
