Problem

AM radio signals can usually be heard behind a hill, but FM often cannot. That is, AM signals bend more than FM. Explain. (Radio signals, as we shall see, are carried by electromagnetic waves whose wavelength for AM is typically 200 to 600 m and for FM about 3 m.)

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    The bending of the wave around an obstacle is called diffraction. The diffraction effect depends when wavelength is comparable to the size of the obstacle.

    The wavelength of AM waves is longer than FM waves. With the longer wavelengths AM radio signals can bend around the hill. The shorter wavelength FM waves are blocked by the hill.

    So, the diffraction of AM waves is possible at the hill. Thus, AM wave signals is heard behind the hill. The wavelength of FM wave is very small compare to the size of the hill. So these waves do not diffract. So FM wave signals cannot hear behind the hill.
