Can the amplitude of the standing waves in Fig. 11â€“40 be greater than the amplitude of the vibrations that cause them (up and down motion of the hand)?

Step-by-step solution
  1. Step 1 of 1

    Standing waves are formed in a cord with one end fixed and the other end is moved. To produce oscillations in the cord, hand of the person moves up and down. These oscillations produce two travelling waves. The interference of two traveling waves results in a standing wave.

    The up and down motion of hand has amplitude produced by a single wave. But for standing wave interference of two waves causes larger amplitude.

    Thus, the standing wave produces greater amplitude than the up and down motion of hand.
